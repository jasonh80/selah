# Chapter-Page Layout Spec (v1 — Mark 6 pilot)

*Owner's UI direction (from earlier ChatGPT planning, re-confirmed 2026-07-15).
Pilot on Mark 6, then apply to Mark 8 / Psalm 23 and become the template for
every chapter. Goal: tighter, more visual, more premium, more memorable —
walking-on-water as the visual anchor, Scripture immediately available at top.*

## Above the fold (target composition)
Mark 6 · "The Shepherd and the Storm" · **Read Mark 6** control ·
Quick Dive / Deep Dive tabs · **Selah Focus** control · collapsed Scripture
preview · walking-on-water key image · visible start of the Visual Chapter Path.

## The 18 changes

1. **Hero swap:** walking-on-water becomes the key image; Nazareth moves into
   the scene sequence.
2. **"Read Mark 6" inline:** chapter hidden by default but available at top;
   clicking expands the full chapter inline beneath the top controls (content
   pushes down — no jump to a lower section).
3. **Collapsed Scripture preview** beneath top controls (first words of the
   selected translation). Expanded state control reads "Hide Mark 6."
4. **Tighten everything:** reduce vertical whitespace, section gaps, card
   padding. Minimal and premium, but denser.
5. **Spacing scale:** 4 / 8 / 12 / 16 / 24 / 32 / 48px. Mobile mostly 12–24px;
   32/48 reserved for major transitions.
6. (Composition — see above-the-fold list.)
7. **Visual Chapter Path** replaces the five-image block: Nazareth → Twelve
   sent → Herod's feast → Feeding 5,000 → Walking on water. Mobile: horizontal
   snap carousel, one full card + deliberate partial next-card preview.
   Desktop: refined rail or asymmetric grid — NOT an equal-card dashboard.
8. **Cinematic images:** larger, intentional; no accidental-looking crops;
   partial views only where they clearly signal swipe.
9. **Expandable images (approved):** click → enlarge from position to
   near-full-screen viewer. v1: close + zoom + pinch + pan, size/source
   limits. Later: swipe between images. Images must not look clickable until
   the behavior works.
10. **Scene Check integrated with its image:** short title may sit over the
    image; long description below or tap-to-expand. No paragraphs over
    images; no hover-dependent behavior.
11. **"People in Mark 6"** compact section near top. Card: headshot · name ·
    approximate age or life stage · role in chapter · scene/verse range when
    useful. Age labels careful ("about 30s," "adult," "age unknown") — never
    invented precision.
12. People for Mark 6: Jesus, the Twelve, John the Baptist, Herod Antipas,
    Herodias, Herodias's daughter, the crowd, the people of Nazareth.
    *(Kelly library gaps: Herod Antipas, Herodias, daughter, crowd/Nazareth
    groups — create minimal profiles; portraits via ChatGPT like the rest.)*
13. **Compact top previews** for People · Where It Fits · Maps & Places; full
    teaching versions stay lower.
14. **Move What Most People Miss + What People Ask higher**, kept adjacent.
15. **Fold Deeper Study + Go Deeper into one Deep Dive system** (render-level
    merge; owner approves the merged structure during the session).
16. **Selah Focus control** near top: reduces interface chrome, dims
    secondary nav — an undistracted study state. (Builds on the existing
    ReadingModeProvider.)
17. **Single-column, mobile-first stays.** No sidebars.
18. **Remove public build/transparency info before launch.** ESV copyright
    attribution remains — owner-confirmed 2026-07-15 (licensing requirement).

## Sequencing

- Mark 7 publish comes first (owner decision — small group).
- Items 1–10, 13–18: the layout session (owner live for taste calls).
- Items 11–12: ride the Kelly integration lane (fixes already approved);
  needs the new Herod-family profiles authored + portraits.
- Spacing system (5) lands as design tokens so Mark 8 and future chapters
