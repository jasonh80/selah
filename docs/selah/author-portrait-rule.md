# The Author Gallery — rule and book-by-book tiers

*Owner ask, 2026-07-23: a photo of the author in the Author section, for every
writer of the Bible we know of.*

**The empty frames are the point.** Where Scripture does not tell us who wrote
a book, Selah shows no face and says so. No other Bible product does this, and
it teaches something true in the place where everyone else quietly invents.

---

## Why the Author card needs its own rule

A face in a *scene* is understood as a depiction. A face in the **Author** card
is a claim about who wrote the book. Mark's Gospel is anonymous — our own
Behind the Chapter card already says "the book itself is anonymous" — so a
portrait captioned *Mark* would assert exactly what the page just admitted we
do not know.

So authorship gets three tiers, and the tier is always visible to the reader.

### Tier A — NAMED IN THE TEXT
The book itself names its author. Portrait renders normally.
Caption: *"Paul — named as the writer in the letter itself."*

### Tier B — TRADITIONAL ATTRIBUTION
The book is anonymous; early tradition names someone. Portrait renders **with
the tradition stated in the caption**, never as fact.
Caption: *"Mark — the book does not name its author. Early Christian testimony
credits John Mark, a companion of Peter."*

### Tier C — UNKNOWN
Nobody knows. **No face.** An empty frame with a plain line.
Caption: *"We don't know who wrote Hebrews. Neither did the earliest readers."*

**Binding rules**
1. A Tier B portrait may never be captioned as if it were Tier A.
2. Tier C never gets a silhouette, a shadowed figure, or a "representative
   writer." An empty frame, honestly labelled. A vague figure is still a claim.
3. Where scholars dispute a Tier A naming (the Pastorals, Revelation's John),
   the portrait represents **the person the text names**, and the caption notes
   the dispute. We depict the claim, not our verdict on it.
4. Portraits are representative depictions like all Selah faces — never
   "this is what he looked like."

## Book-by-book

### Old Testament

| Books | Tier | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Genesis–Deuteronomy | **B** | Anonymous. Mosaic authorship is ancient tradition; the text does record Moses writing (Exod 24:4; Deut 31:9, 24). ✅ Moses is cast. |
| Joshua | **C** | Anonymous. Josh 24:26 has Joshua writing *some* words. ✅ profile exists, portrait not cast. |
| Judges · Ruth | **C** | Anonymous. |
| 1–2 Samuel | **C** | Anonymous — and note Samuel dies partway through 1 Samuel. |
| 1–2 Kings | **C** | Anonymous. |
| 1–2 Chronicles | **C** | Anonymous; Ezra is a common traditional guess, weakly held. |
| Ezra | **A** | First-person memoir sections. |
| Nehemiah | **A** | "The words of Nehemiah" (1:1). |
| Esther | **C** | Anonymous. ✅ Esther is profiled but she is the subject, not the author. |
| Job | **C** | Anonymous, and probably the oldest mystery in the canon. |
| Psalms | **A / C, per psalm** | Superscriptions name David, Asaph, the sons of Korah, Solomon, Heman, Ethan, and **Moses (Ps 90)**. Many psalms name nobody. The gallery should follow the psalm, not the book. ✅ David, Moses, Solomon cast or profiled. |
| Proverbs | **A** | Solomon (1:1), plus **Agur** (30:1) and **Lemuel** (31:1) — two named writers almost nobody can picture. |
| Ecclesiastes | **B** | "The Preacher, son of David" — Solomon by tradition; the book never names him. |
| Song of Songs | **A** | "of Solomon" (1:1), though the Hebrew preposition is debatable — note it. |
| Isaiah | **A** | 1:1. ✅ profiled. |
| Jeremiah | **A** | 1:1. |
| Lamentations | **B** | Anonymous; tradition credits Jeremiah. |
| Ezekiel | **A** | 1:3. |
| Daniel | **A** | First-person from ch. 7 onward. ✅ profiled. |
| Hosea · Joel · Amos · Obadiah · Micah · Nahum · Habakkuk · Zephaniah · Haggai · Zechariah · Malachi | **A** | Each names its prophet in the opening line. |
| Jonah | **C** | The book is *about* Jonah, told in the third person. It never claims he wrote it. **A good teaching moment.** |

## What this means for casting

**Already have portraits:** Moses, David, Paul, Matthew, Peter.
**Profiled, portrait needed:** Solomon, Isaiah, Daniel, John, James the Just.
**Not yet profiled, needed for Tier A/B:** Mark, Luke, Jeremiah, Ezekiel,
Nehemiah, Ezra, Jude, the eleven named minor prophets, Agur, Lemuel, Asaph.

That is a normal casting batch, and it runs under the existing rules — Mind
before generation, Eyes after selection, the eleven differentiator axes so a
row of prophets does not become one bearded man repeated eleven times.

**Nothing is needed for Tier C.** That is the cheapest and best part of the
feature.

- the portrait (Tier A/B) or an empty frame (Tier C)
