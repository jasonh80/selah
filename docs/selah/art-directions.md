# Art Directions — a running list

*Owner ask, 2026-07-23: "be thinking of what art we can create throughout this
project. interesting visuals."*

Not a build queue. A place ideas live so they do not evaporate. Each entry
notes whether it is buildable with what we have.

---

## The through-line

**Selah's signature is honest data made beautiful.** Nobody else in Bible
publishing makes *uncertainty* look good — every other map draws a confident
line it cannot support. Our fading territory map, our dashed debated-area
badges, our "areas, not spots" rule: these started as honesty constraints and
they are turning into a visual language. Lean into that. The restraint is the
style.

Second through-line: **the true-time timeline** already proved that a plain
true fact — equal distance equals equal years — is more moving than decoration.
The empty stretches teach. Look for more facts that carry their own weight.

---

## 1. Things you could have held

The Mark 12 denarius shot is the template: an object rendered exactly, very
close, no people, shallow focus. We can be *precise* here in a way we can never
be about faces, and precision is its own beauty.

- the widow's two lepta — the smallest coins in circulation, at true scale
- a mustard seed at true scale beside a hand
- a first-century fishing net, wet, in a working hand
- a millstone (Mark 9:42) — its real weight is the whole point
- a Roman nail
- an alabaster jar, unbroken
- barley loaves and dried fish, as actually eaten

**Buildable now.** Highest accuracy-per-effort images we can make.

## 2. Scale you can feel

Numbers in Scripture are usually read past. Draw them.

- five thousand as five thousand marks
- the temple's real footprint over a modern stadium
- the distance Jesus walks across Mark, drawn against something known
- thirty pieces of silver as thirty actual coins
- the depth of the Sea of Galilee against the height of the storm

**Buildable now** as data graphics, no generation needed.

## 3. Then and now, same frame

A satellite photo of Capernaum today with the first-century street plan
ghosted over it. The reader sees the actual ground. We already have the imagery
and the coordinates.

**Needs authored overlay data.** Honesty gate: only where excavation supports
the plan, and the ghost must read as a reconstruction, not a photograph.

## 4. Real light, real season

Scripture often gives the hour, and the feasts date the season. Render the
scene at that hour, in that month's light and weather — the rains, the harvest,
the heat. Most Bible art is lit at a generic golden hour regardless of what the
text says.

**Buildable now** — it is prompt discipline, not new tooling. Should become an
Eyes rule.

## 5. The family with faces

The genealogy as a line of real portraits from our cast, so Jesus' ancestors
stop being a list of names. Ties the character database to the timeline.

**Blocked on cast coverage** — most of the genealogy is uncast. Long horizon.

## 6. Uncertainty as a visual language

Formalise what we are already doing so a reader learns to read our confidence
at a glance: solid = attested, dashed = debated, fading = unknown edge, absent =
we do not know. Used consistently across maps, timelines, and reconstructions,
it becomes a thing only Selah has.

**Buildable now** as a spec; Eyes v1.3 material.

## 7. The whole-Bible route overlay

Every journey in Scripture on one map, filterable by era or person. Recorded in
`territory-map-proposal.md` §6 as a data constraint so it stays free.

## 8. The era palette — cool to warm, arriving at Jesus

*Owner idea, 2026-07-23: "starts with cool blue colors and each era has a
different warmer color… something like that."*

One palette that warms as the story moves forward, shared by the timeline rail
and the route overlay so the reader learns it once and it means the same thing
in both places.

| Era | Colour |
|---|---|
| Creation → flood | deep cool blue |
| Patriarchs | teal |
| Exodus → conquest | green |
| Judges → monarchy | gold |
| Exile → return | amber |
| **Jesus** | **red** |
| Apostles → church | warm ember, cooling slightly |

**Why this works rather than merely looking nice:** the box law already
reserves red for Jesus and nothing else. A scale that warms toward the present
therefore *arrives* at red exactly where the story arrives at Him — the
existing constraint supplies the climax for free, and no other era may borrow
that colour.

**Guardrails:** colour must never be the only signal (accessibility — pair with
label, shape, or position); the palette encodes *era*, never *importance* or
*moral weight*; and it must stay legible in both themes.

**Buildable now** as a token set; the timeline is the natural first user.
