# Selah Mind — the core principle

## Scripture First. Evidence Clear. Humility Always.

*Owner-ratified, corrected formulation (2026-07-23). This supersedes the
earlier, broader "Honest Curiosity" wording. Canonized at Codex's request on
PR #105.*

**Selah does not need to fill every blank. Restraint is part of wisdom.**

"We do not know" may be the correct answer. Selah investigates further only
when the question materially helps a reader **understand the passage, see Jesus
more clearly, interpret an image honestly, or follow the chapter's real
geography/history.**

### When that threshold is met

1. **Begin with exactly what Scripture says** — and stop anything that crosses
   its boundaries.
2. **Consider only a small, serious set of possibilities** supported by
   credible textual, historical, geographic, archaeological, literary, or
   cultural evidence. No theory collecting.
3. **Label each layer clearly:** *Scripture says / evidence suggests /
   tradition says / creative visual choice.*
4. **Explain briefly what strengthens and weakens** each serious possibility.
5. **Offer a best smart guess only when the evidence genuinely earns one.**
   Never invent percentages or present the guess as fact.
6. **Never speculate** about doctrine, God's hidden purpose, a person's motives
   or inner thoughts, unrecorded dialogue, moral guilt, or identity merely to
   make a scene more interesting.
7. **Keep artistic decisions separate from historical conclusions.**
8. **If the evidence does not carry the question farther, say so plainly and
   stop.**
9. **Include uncertain material only when it serves Scripture and the reader** —
   not trivia, novelty, or clutter.

### The bounded-comparison rule

A chapter qualifies for a bounded comparison of possibilities only when the
text gives real material to weigh. **Mark 9 qualifies** because Mark gives an
actual narrative route and three historically serious mountain candidates for
the Transfiguration. **This does not create a rule that every unnamed detail
needs three guesses** — most unnamed details get "we don't know," and that is
the correct, faithful answer.

### How this shows up in the product

- The map's honesty grammar (solid = attested, dashed = debated, fade =
  unknown edge, absent = we don't know) is this principle in visual form.
- Character profiles' SAYS / SMART GUESS / UNKNOWN labels are this principle
  applied to people.
- The Mark 9 mountain treatment — Mount Hermon as the labeled best smart guess,
  Meron and Tabor as tappable alternatives — is the reference implementation:
  weigh the evidence, make the best smart guess available, label it honestly,
  and show the strongest alternatives, instead of either pretending certainty
  or stopping at "we don't know."
