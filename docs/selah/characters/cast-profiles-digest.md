## Provenance labels (Codex #105 review, 2026-07-23)

Every claim in these profiles is one of three things, and the profile must say
which. Accurate-sounding prose is not enough.

- **SAYS** — Scripture states it.
- **SMART GUESS** — a defensible inference from occupation, era, geography, or
  long-standing church tradition. It is Selah's reasoning, not the Bible's
  statement, and the reader must never meet it as biography.
- **UNKNOWN** — the text is silent. Depictions in this space are
  *representative*: one plausible person, not the person.

Rules that follow from it:
- Traditional identifications (Matthew = Levi, Bartholomew = Nathanael,
  Thaddaeus = Judas son of James) are **SMART GUESS**, not SAYS.
- Later-life claims — missions, journeys, deaths, martyrdoms, dates — are
  **SMART GUESS (tradition)** unless Scripture records them. Acts 12:2 records
  James son of Zebedee's death; almost nothing else does.
- Exact ages are **SMART GUESS** everywhere. Scripture gives no disciple's age.
- Build and complexion inferred from occupation are **SMART GUESS
  (representative depiction)**. A tax collector's work makes a soft, indoor
  build *plausible*; it does not make it a fact about Matthew.

## Andrew (`andrew`)
- **Occupation:** Commercial fisherman (SAYS, Mark 1:16); earlier a disciple of John the Baptist (SAYS, John 1:35–40)
- **Build:** SMART GUESS from the trade — broad-shouldered and rope-callused from nets, oars, and hauled catches; a laborer's strength, never sculpted.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a sun-weathered 1st-century Galilean: olive-brown skin, dark hair, full dark beard, working hands. Cast a distinct face from Peter — brothers, not twins. No injury, scarring, or hardship backstory: none is recorded.
- **Life stages:** Fisherman of Bethsaida and Capernaum · First to Follow · Disciple of the Twelve · Apostle (later years and death UNKNOWN; tradition only)
- **Why they matter:** The first-called apostle and the quiet pattern of evangelism — before he does anything else, Andrew finds his brother and brings him to Jesus (SAYS).

## James (son of Zebedee) (`james-zebedee`)
- **Occupation:** Commercial fisherman in the family business — a boat with hired servants (SAYS, Mark 1:20); apostle
- **Build:** SMART GUESS from the trade — broad and thick through the back and arms; functional, weathered, never sculpted.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a sun-weathered Galilean fisherman with a family resemblance to his brother John; heavier-boned and squarer through the jaw, the elder of the two (birth-order reading is SMART GUESS).
- **Life stages:** Fisherman · Called, Named "Sons of Thunder" (SAYS, Mark 3:17) · Inner-Circle Disciple (SAYS) · Apostle in Jerusalem · **Martyred by Herod (SAYS, Acts 12:2)** — the one death among the Twelve Scripture records; the year (~AD 44) is SMART GUESS.
- **Why they matter:** The first of the Twelve to die for Jesus — the Son of Thunder who asked for a throne beside Christ and received the cup instead.

## Matthew (Levi) (`matthew`)
- **Occupation:** Tax collector at Capernaum (SAYS, Matt 9:9 / Mark 2:14). The identification of Matthew with Levi is **SMART GUESS** — long-held and well-supported, but the Gospels do not equate the names outright.
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — plausibly softer and less weathered than the fishermen beside him, the body of seated work. Not a fact about Matthew.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a Galilean Jew of the customs house: olive-brown skin, dark hair, trimmed beard. **Wardrobe simplified per the Eyes certification:** ordinary Galilean dress, no ledger props, no ink-stained hands, no costume signalling of his trade.
- **Life stages:** Tax Collector of Capernaum · Called from the Booth (SAYS) · One of the Twelve (SAYS) · Apostle and Evangelist (later ministry, death, and the Gospel's authorship attribution are SMART GUESS/tradition)
- **Why they matter:** The despised toll-man Jesus called by name — proof that no reputation puts anyone beyond "Follow me."

## Thomas (Didymus) (`thomas`)
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN.** Scripture records no trade for Thomas. Depict him as an ordinary Galilean working man and claim nothing further.
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — ordinary working build for the era; nothing sculpted, nothing heroic.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an ordinary 1st-century Galilean Jewish man. His name means "twin" (SAYS); Scripture never names the sibling (UNKNOWN).
- **Life stages:** Called Among the Twelve (SAYS) · "Let us also go, that we may die with him" (SAYS, John 11:16) · Doubt and Confession (SAYS, John 20) · Apostle to the East — **SMART GUESS (tradition); the mission, the date, and the death are not recorded.**
- **Why they matter:** The honest doubter whose demand to see the wounds ended in the highest confession any disciple speaks in the Gospels.

## Philip (`philip`)
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN** — Scripture records no trade. From Bethsaida (SAYS, John 1:44).
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — ordinary, sturdy, unspecialized.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an everyday Galilean townsman's face.
- **Life stages:** Called at the Jordan (SAYS) · One of the Twelve (SAYS) · Passion Week questions (SAYS, John 12/14) · Later ministry and "Elder of Hierapolis" — **SMART GUESS (tradition), and the tradition may conflate him with Philip the Evangelist of Acts.** Ages in this stage are guesses; do not render him at the aging rule's extreme old-age face on the strength of a tradition.
- **Why they matter:** The disciple who did the math — his honest arithmetic at the feeding measures how far human resources fall short of what Jesus is about to do.

## Bartholomew (Nathanael) (`bartholomew`)
- **Conditional identity (Codex #105):** Bartholomew = Nathanael is a
  **SMART GUESS** harmonization of the Synoptic lists with John's Gospel — not
  Scripture. **Everything John tells us about *Nathanael* is therefore
  CONDITIONAL on that guess when applied to Bartholomew**, and none of it may be
  labeled unconditional SAYS for this cast member.
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN.** Cana is *Nathanael's* town (John 21:2 — SAYS of Nathanael, conditional here).
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — ordinary and unspecialized; nothing reads as a trade.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an ordinary Galilean man with an open, unguarded face. The "an Israelite indeed, in whom there is no deceit" line (John 1:47) is Jesus' word about *Nathanael* and carries into the portrait only through the harmonization — a character note, not a physical description and not unconditional SAYS.
- **Life stages:** Called under the Fig Tree (SAYS of *Nathanael*, conditional) · One of the Twelve (SAYS) · Witness of the Risen Lord (SAYS) · Apostle to the East — **SMART GUESS (tradition; undated, and the martyrdom accounts are late).**
- **Why they matter:** The honest skeptic won in a single sentence — proof that Jesus knows a person completely before they ever approach him.

## James (son of Alphaeus) (`james-alphaeus`)
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN.**
- **Whole profile is a SMART GUESS.** Scripture records his name in the apostle lists and nothing else — no word, no deed, no age, no death. Everything visual here is a *representative* Galilean man of the era, deliberately unremarkable, and the profile must say so wherever it is used.
- **Build / Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — ordinary working build, a face the crowd would not pick out.
- **Life stages:** Called to the Twelve (SAYS) · Sent Out Two by Two (SAYS of the Twelve as a group) · Witness of the Risen Lord (SAYS of the group) · Later Ministry — **SMART GUESS (tradition), and the traditions disagree with each other.**
- **Why they matter:** The quietest of the Twelve — not one recorded word or deed, yet named in every list and sent with the same authority.

## Thaddaeus (Judas son of James) (`thaddaeus`)
- **Conditional identity (Codex #105):** Thaddaeus (Matthew/Mark) = Judas son
  of James (Luke/John) is a **SMART GUESS** harmonization, not Scripture. Any
  material drawn from the "Judas (not Iscariot)" texts is CONDITIONAL on that
  guess and may not be labeled unconditional SAYS for this cast member. His
  appearance is representative regardless.
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN.**
- **Build / Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an ordinary Galilean man, deliberately unremarkable.
- **Life stages:** Called into the Twelve (SAYS) · Disciple on the Road · The Question in the Upper Room (John 14:22 — SAYS of "Judas, not Iscariot," conditional on the identification) · Apostle — **death UNKNOWN; the c. AD 65 martyrdom is late tradition and must be labeled as such, never dated as fact.**
- **Why they matter:** The quietest of the Twelve asked one question at the last supper — and drew out Jesus' promise to make his home with everyone who loves him.

## Simon the Zealot (`simon-zealot`)
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN.** Scripture gives only the designation "the Zealot" / "the Cananaean" (SAYS, Luke 6:15; Mark 3:18). **That word does not give us a life story.** It may describe zeal for the law rather than membership in an armed movement, and the organized Zealot party as such is generally dated to the revolt of AD 66 — after his call. **Corrected 2026-07-23: the former "armed nationalist / hill fugitive / freedom fighter who would have targeted Matthew" biography is removed from the canon. It was invention.**
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an ordinary working man of the era.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an ordinary Galilean: Levantine features, dark hair, close beard, alert and attentive. **Never depict weapons, armour, military boots, a hunted or fugitive look, nationalist symbols, or wilderness-survivor styling.** (This is why Simon is the one true re-audition on the neutral brief.)
- **Life stages:** Before his call — **UNKNOWN, and must be left blank rather than filled in** · Called to the Twelve (SAYS) · Disciple of the Ministry Years (SAYS of the group) · Upper Room Witness (SAYS of the group)
- **Why they matter:** He and Matthew sat at the same table under the same Lord. Say it that way — as two men from opposite ends of a divided people — without narrating a war record Scripture never gives him.

## Herod Antipas (`herod-antipas`)
- **Occupation:** Tetrarch of Galilee and Perea under Rome (SAYS + Josephus); courtier and city-builder (Josephus)
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a sedentary, well-fed court body is *plausible* for a client ruler; it is not recorded. Cast it as a choice, not a compulsory body type, and avoid making the body itself the moral verdict.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a Levantine ruler in Greco-Roman court fashion, groomed and barbered. The "hands that have never worked" detail is invention; drop it.
- **Life stages:** Herod's Heir · Builder Tetrarch · John's Executioner (SAYS, Mark 6) · Haunted Ruler, Mocking Judge (SAYS, Mark 6:16; Luke 23:11) · Deposed and Exiled (Josephus). Ages are SMART GUESS throughout.
- **Why they matter:** The tetrarch who ruled the Galilee of Jesus' entire ministry and liked listening to John right up until he beheaded him over a dinner-party oath — the standing warning that enjoying the truth is not the same as obeying it.

## Herodias (`herodias`)
- **Occupation:** Herodian princess and the tetrarch's wife (SAYS + Josephus)
- **Age:** **~46 at Mark 6** (corrected 2026-07-23 per the Eyes certification; the earlier range was wrong for the chapter). Still a SMART GUESS built from Josephus's chronology.
- **Build:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a palace-kept frame. The litter-bearers detail is invention; drop it.
- **Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — an aristocratic Levantine woman, dark hair dressed and pinned, skin unweathered. **Guardrail stands: never cast or light her as a seductress-villain.** Her recorded sin is a nursed grudge, not allure.
- **Life stages:** Princess of Herod's House · Wife of Herod II (SAYS/Josephus) · The Unlawful Marriage (SAYS, Mark 6:17–18) · The Grudge and the Platter (SAYS, Mark 6:19–28) · Exile (Josephus)
- **Why they matter:** The nursed grudge with a crown — resentment against the voice that names your sin will wait years for one opportune banquet.

## The Syrophoenician Woman (`syrophoenician-woman`)
- **Identity:** **UNKNOWN — she is unnamed.** A Greek, Syrophoenician by birth (SAYS, Mark 7:26). Every visual is explicitly a **representative depiction**, and the People page must label it that way.
- **Age:** **UNSPECIFIED.** The "~30" in the profile is a casting placeholder for a mother of a young child, not an estimate of her age (corrected 2026-07-23).
- **Occupation:** **UNKNOWN** beyond "mother" (SAYS). The water-carrying and housework detail is SMART GUESS from the era; do not state it as hers.
- **Build / Appearance:** SMART GUESS (representative) — a Levantine-Phoenician woman, dark hair covered, worn by a sick child's long nights.
- **Life stages:** All stages are the single Mark 7 encounter, seen in moments — not a biography.
- **Why they matter:** A Gentile outsider with no claim but faith takes Jesus' hard word and hands it back as the answer he praises — proof, before the mission to the nations begins, that crumbs from Israel's table are enough to heal.
