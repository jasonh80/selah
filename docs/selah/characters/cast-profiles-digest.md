## Andrew (`andrew`)
- **Occupation:** Commercial fisherman; disciple of John the Baptist, then apostle of Jesus
- **Build:** Broad-shouldered and rope-callused from a working life of nets, oars, and hauled catches — a laborer's strength, never sculpted.
- **Appearance:** A sun-weathered 1st-century Galilean fisherman: olive-brown skin, dark hair and a full dark beard, strong hands scarred and callused by wet rope and net-mending. A distinct face from Peter — brothers, not twins — leaner-featured and quieter-eyed beside his bolder, broader brother.
- **Life stages:** Fisherman of Bethsaida and Capernaum (~20–25, looks ~20–25) · First to Follow (~25, looks ~25) · Disciple of the Twelve (~25–31, looks ~25–32) · Apostle and Martyr (~30–65, looks ~31–80)
- **Why they matter:** The first-called apostle and the quiet pattern of evangelism — before he does anything else, Andrew finds his brother and brings him to Jesus.

## James (son of Zebedee) (`james-zebedee`)
- **Occupation:** Commercial fisherman in the family business (a boat with hired servants, Mark 1:20); later apostle
- **Build:** Broad-shouldered and thick through the back and arms from years of hauling nets, rowing, and dragging catches ashore — a working fisherman's functional power, weathered and slightly asymmetrical, never sculpted.
- **Appearance:** A broad, sun-weathered Galilean fisherman, mid-twenties at his call: olive-brown skin, dark hair, a full dark beard, rope-callused hands and salt-cracked knuckles. A visible family resemblance to his younger brother John, but heavier-boned, squarer through the jaw, and harder-edged in expression — the elder of the two.
- **Life stages:** Fisherman (~22–25, looks ~22–25) · Called, Named Thunder (~25, looks ~25) · Inner-Circle Disciple (~25–30, looks ~25–31) · Apostle in Jerusalem (~30–41, looks ~31–43) · First Martyr of the Twelve (~41, looks ~43)
- **Why they matter:** The first of the Twelve to die for Jesus — the Son of Thunder who asked for a throne beside Christ and received the cup instead, proof that following costs exactly what Jesus said it would.

## Matthew (Levi) (`matthew`)
- **Occupation:** Tax collector (customs/toll officer) at Capernaum under Herod Antipas; later apostle
- **Build:** Sedentary and soft next to the fishermen — the body of a man who sat at a booth all day, not one who hauled nets; smooth, ink-stained ledger hands without a laborer's calluses or shoulders.
- **Appearance:** A Galilean Jew of the customs house: olive-brown skin, dark hair and a trimmed beard, softer-faced and paler than the fishermen beside him — a man who worked seated, in shade, with a pen.
- **Life stages:** Tax Collector of Capernaum (~26–28, looks ~27–29) · Called from the Booth (~28, looks ~29) · One of the Twelve (~28–33, looks ~29–34) · Apostle and Evangelist (~33–65 (traditions vary), looks ~34–80)
- **Why they matter:** The despised toll-man Jesus called by name — living proof that no reputation puts anyone beyond 'Follow me,' and the apostle whose name the church attached to its first-placed Gospel.

## Thomas (Didymus) (`thomas`)
- **Occupation:** Unrecorded — an ordinary Galilean working man; disciple, then apostle of Jesus
- **Build:** Ordinary working-man's build for the era — serviceable strength and work-worn hands from daily labor, nothing sculpted or heroic.
- **Appearance:** An ordinary 1st-century Galilean Jewish man: olive-brown skin, dark hair, short dark beard, weathered by outdoor village life. Nothing in the text sets him apart physically — his name means 'twin,' but Scripture never names the sibling.
- **Life stages:** Called Among the Twelve (~25–30, looks ~25–31) · Ready to Die with Him (~28–32, looks ~29–33) · Doubt and Confession (~28–33, looks ~29–34) · Apostle to the East (~30–70 (death c. AD 72, per tradition), looks ~31–100)
- **Why they matter:** The honest doubter whose demand to see the wounds ended in the highest confession any disciple speaks in the Gospels — proof that hard questions, brought to Jesus, can end in worship.

## Philip (`philip`)
- **Occupation:** Unrecorded — a working man of a Galilean fishing town; one of the Twelve, then apostle
- **Build:** Ordinary working build of a fishing-town man — sturdy and serviceable, hands roughened by daily labor; Scripture records no trade for him, so nothing sculpted, nothing heroic.
- **Appearance:** An ordinary Galilean townsman: olive-brown skin, dark hair and beard, Levantine features — an everyday face from Bethsaida's harborside, the kind strangers find easy to approach.
- **Life stages:** Called at the Jordan (~25, looks ~25) · One of the Twelve (~25–28, looks ~25–29) · Passion Week Questions (~28, looks ~29) · Apostle and Witness (~28–35, looks ~29–36) · Elder of Hierapolis (tradition) (~75–80 (tradition), looks ~120)
- **Why they matter:** The disciple who did the math — his honest arithmetic at the feeding measures exactly how far human resources fall short of what Jesus is about to do, and his 'Come and see' remains the simplest invitation to Jesus ever spoken.

## Bartholomew (Nathanael) (`bartholomew`)
- **Occupation:** Unrecorded in Scripture — an ordinary Galilean villager of Cana; later apostle and missionary
- **Build:** Ordinary working-man's build for a Galilean village — capable, sturdy, unremarkable. His occupation is unrecorded, so nothing about him reads specialized: not a fisherman's shoulders, not a scholar's softness, and never sculpted or heroic.
- **Appearance:** An ordinary 1st-century Galilean Jewish man of Cana: olive-brown skin, dark hair and eyes, a short dark beard, and an open, unguarded face — nothing hidden in it. This is the man Jesus read at a glance under the fig tree and named 'an Israelite indeed, in whom there is no deceit.'
- **Life stages:** Called under the Fig Tree (~22–25, looks ~22–25) · One of the Twelve (~22–30, looks ~22–31) · Witness of the Risen Lord (~25–30, looks ~25–31) · Apostle to the East (tradition) (~35–65 (tradition; undated), looks ~36–80)
- **Why they matter:** The honest skeptic won in a single sentence — living proof that Jesus knows a person completely before they ever approach him, and that doubt without guile can leap straight to confession.

## James (son of Alphaeus) (`james-alphaeus`)
- **Occupation:** Unrecorded — ordinary Galilean labor; apostle of the Twelve
- **Build:** Ordinary working build for the era — hands used to daily labor, serviceable and unremarkable; nothing sculpted, nothing heroic.
- **Appearance:** An ordinary Galilean workingman: olive-brown skin, dark hair and beard, a face the crowd would not pick out — the kind the record never pauses to describe.
- **Life stages:** Called to the Twelve (~25–30, looks ~25–31) · Sent Out Two by Two (~26–31, looks ~27–32) · Witness of the Risen Lord (~27–32, looks ~28–33) · Later Ministry (tradition) (~35–60, looks ~36–64)
- **Why they matter:** The quietest of the Twelve — not one recorded word or deed, yet named in every list and sent with the same authority: proof that Jesus keeps full count of the faithful whom history does not.

## Thaddaeus (Judas son of James) (`thaddaeus`)
- **Occupation:** Unrecorded — an ordinary Galilean working man before his call; then apostle, one of the Twelve
- **Build:** Ordinary working build of a Galilean villager — serviceable shoulders and work-worn hands from unrecorded daily labor; neither a fisherman's breadth nor a scribe's softness, and never sculpted or heroic.
- **Appearance:** An ordinary 1st-century Galilean Jewish man: olive-brown sun-weathered skin, dark eyes, short dark hair and a short dark beard; a face that would draw no second glance in a Capernaum crowd — Scripture records nothing that sets his appearance apart, and that ordinariness is the point.
- **Life stages:** Called into the Twelve (~25 (age unrecorded; a young Galilean adult), looks ~25) · Disciple on the Road (~25–28, looks ~25–29) · The Question in the Upper Room (~28–30, looks ~29–31) · Witness and Apostle (~30–62 (death undated; tradition places martyrdom c. AD 65), looks ~31–68)
- **Why they matter:** The quietest of the Twelve asked one question at the last supper — and drew out Jesus' promise to make his home with everyone who loves him.

## Simon the Zealot (`simon-zealot`)
- **Occupation:** Former armed nationalist of the zealot movement; then apostle of Jesus
- **Build:** Wiry and hardened — lean, quick, rough-country strong from years living light in the hills; stringy, functional muscle and weathered hands, nothing of the gymnasium about him.
- **Appearance:** A wiry, sun-hardened Galilean: Levantine features, dark hair and a close beard, and the constant scanning watchfulness of a man who spent years expecting Roman boots at the door.
- **Life stages:** Zealot Years (~20–26, looks ~20–27) · Called to the Twelve (~27, looks ~28) · Disciple of the Ministry Years (~27–30, looks ~28–31) · Upper Room Witness (~30, looks ~31)
- **Why they matter:** Living proof of what Jesus' table does to enmity — the freedom fighter seated beside the tax collector he would once have marked, both made apostles of the same Lord.

## Herod Antipas (`herod-antipas`)
- **Occupation:** Tetrarch — Rome's client ruler of Galilee and Perea; courtier and city-builder
- **Build:** Banquet-soft: the padded, sedentary body of a man whose working life is reclining at table — fleshy through the middle and under the jaw, carried with a ruler's practiced poise. Never martial, never lean.
- **Appearance:** A well-fed Levantine ruler: olive skin over Idumean-Jewish features, dark hair and beard barbered close in the Greco-Roman court fashion, oiled and groomed, with the manicured hands of a man who has never worked.
- **Life stages:** Herod's Heir (~14–17, looks ~14–17) · Builder Tetrarch (~17–45, looks ~17–47) · John's Executioner (~47–48, looks ~49–51) · Haunted Ruler, Mocking Judge (~49–52, looks ~52–55) · Deposed and Exiled (~58+, looks ~61+)
- **Why they matter:** The tetrarch who ruled the Galilee of Jesus' entire ministry and liked listening to John right up until he beheaded him over a dinner-party oath — the standing warning that enjoying the truth is not the same as obeying it.

## Herodias (`herodias`)
- **Occupation:** Royal princess and tetrarch's consort; her work was the court — alliance, banquet, and intrigue
- **Build:** Soft-bodied and palace-kept — a frame shaped by banquet couches and litter-bearers, not labor; held erect with rigid, regal control.
- **Appearance:** An aristocratic Levantine woman of Hasmonean-Herodian blood: olive skin kept fine and unweathered, dark eyes lined with kohl, dark hair dressed high in braids and gold pins — beauty carefully maintained.
- **Life stages:** Princess of Herod's House (child–~14, looks child–~14) · Wife of Herod II (~15–40, looks ~15–42) · The Unlawful Marriage (~42, looks ~44) · The Grudge and the Platter (~44, looks ~46) · Ambition's Exile (~54, looks ~57)
- **Why they matter:** Herodias is the nursed grudge with a crown — proof that resentment against the voice that names your sin will wait years for one opportune banquet, and pay with a prophet's head.

## The Syrophoenician Woman (`syrophoenician-woman`)
- **Occupation:** Householder and mother in Tyre's rural hinterland
- **Build:** An ordinary working woman's build — hands and forearms practiced from housework, water-carrying, and child-rearing; sturdy, neither delicate nor athletic.
- **Appearance:** A Levantine-Phoenician woman around thirty: olive skin, dark hair kept covered, a face marked by the long nights of a sick child's mother.
- **Life stages:** Syrophoenician by Birth (child, looks child) · A Mother's Desperation (adult (~30, representative), looks ~31) · The Bold Answer (adult (~30, representative), looks ~31) · The Child Made Whole (adult (~30, representative), looks ~31)
- **Why they matter:** A Gentile outsider with no claim but faith, she takes Jesus' hard word and hands it back as the answer he praises — proof, before the mission to the nations ever begins, that crumbs from Israel's table are enough to heal.
