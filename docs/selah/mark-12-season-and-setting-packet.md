## 1. SCRIPTURE SAYS (locked — stated or required by the text)

- **Place:** the **temple / temple courts** in Jerusalem. Jesus is "walking in
  the temple" (11:27) and teaching "in the temple" (12:35); he sits "opposite
  the treasury" and watches the crowd give (12:41).
- **Public setting:** a **crowd** is present and listening — "the great throng
  heard him gladly" (12:37); the leaders fear the crowd (12:12).
- **Money / commerce on hand:** a **denarius** is produced on the spot (12:15–16);
  people are putting money into the **treasury**, "many rich people put in large
  sums" (12:41); a poor widow puts in "two small copper coins" (12:42).
- **Passion-week frame:** this is the sequence of temple confrontations in the
  days before the Passover of Jesus' death (continues from 11:1–11, the entry;
  the plot to kill him is already active, 11:18, 12:12).
- **Time of day:** not stated for Mark 12's scenes. (Do not assert one.)

## 2. SMART GUESS (grounded inference, labeled — confidence noted)

- **Season: early spring, near Passover — HIGH.** Mark's own Passion sequence
  and the Passover frame (14:1) place these days in **Nisan (March–April)**.
  (Note: the day-count is Mark's narrative sequence, not a dated calendar.)
- **Climate feel: mild days, cool-to-cold nights — HIGH for the range, not a
  number.** Jerusalem sits at **~2,500 ft / ~760 m**. Modern March–April normals
  there run roughly **low-60s to low-70s °F by day, upper-40s to mid-50s °F at
  night**; ancient year-to-year swings and rain remain possible. Mark's own
  detail that Peter later warms himself at a courtyard fire (14:54, 14:67)
  independently supports a genuinely cool night. **Use a broad range; never a
  single reconstructed temperature.**
- **Crowding: heavy — HIGH.** A pilgrim Passover swelled Jerusalem far beyond
  its residents; the temple courts were the largest public gathering space.
- **Sensory: dust, incense/sacrificial smoke, animals, coin-noise, roasting
  food — MEDIUM-HIGH.** Standard, well-attested texture of a working temple at
  festival; not chapter-specific claims, so keep them as atmosphere.
- **Light: hard sun on pale limestone — MEDIUM.** Typical of a bright Judean
  spring day; the text does not fix the hour, so keep light general.

## 3. OPEN CHOICE (text is silent — honest palette, pick per image)

- **Sky/weather of any given scene:** clear spring sun, high thin cloud, or a
  passing shower are all plausible for Nisan. Choose per image; never claim it.
- **Exact hour / shadow length:** unfixed — morning-bright or afternoon are both
  defensible. Do not imply a specific time.
- **Whether the day was warm or on the cooler edge:** within the range above,
  either is honest.

## 4. AVOID

- A **reconstructed temperature as fact** ("it was 68°F"). Range only.
- **Rain, storm, or a visible moon** presented as certain — none is stated.
- **Golden-glow reverence** / empty-serene-temple cliché. This is a **crowded,
  noisy, working** religious complex, and a contested one.
- **Herod's temple drawn as Solomon's** — it is the Herodian complex.
- **Parallel-Gospel weather or timing** smuggled in as though Mark said it.
- **Warm sunny beige default** as the only look across every Mark 12 image.
